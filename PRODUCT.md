# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Thesis examiners, researchers, and urban-planning readers inspecting food-environment evidence, methodology, spatial labels, and intervention scenarios. Their primary task is to understand what the data supports, compare Bengaluru with Mysuru, and avoid mistaking projections for local ground truth.

## Product Purpose

Present the Bengaluru food-environment thesis as an inspectable research dashboard and expose a scientifically honest OSM-derived Mysuru projection. Success means users can trace labels to evidence, see uncertainty and unavailable dimensions, and interpret scenario changes without believing an unsupported model or four-class transfer is running.

## Brand Personality

Academic, precise, restrained. The interface should feel credible and calm, with plain methodological language and enough visual hierarchy to make dense evidence legible.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS landing page, a glossy AI prediction demo, or a map that uses confidence styling to conceal missing evidence. Avoid decorative spectacle, forced certainty, fabricated metrics, and UI copy that outruns the provenance.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before certainty: show the source and evidence level with every projected claim.
- Unknown is a result: preserve data gaps instead of forcing a complete class palette.
- Inspectability over persuasion: make node features, thresholds, and risk flags easy to audit.
- Separate observation, proxy, and scenario: use consistent language and visual treatment for each.
- Keep the map in service of the research question, not as decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard-operable controls. Never rely on color alone to distinguish observed, proxy, and unknown evidence; respect reduced-motion preferences and use explicit text for unavailable values.
